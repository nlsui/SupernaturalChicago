## Schnellreferenz

### Creature Creation Checklist

- [ ] Choose concept and role (predator, herbivore, sapient, etc.)
- [ ] Determine size category (human-sized, larger, smaller)
- [ ] Assign characteristics (STR, CON, SIZ, DEX, INT/INS, POW, CHA)
- [ ] Calculate attributes (Action Points, Damage Modifier, Magic Points, Movement, Initiative)
- [ ] Select 1-3 special abilities
- [ ] Choose appropriate skills (base +20-40%)
- [ ] Design combat style and weapons
- [ ] Determine armor and hit locations
- [ ] Add magic if appropriate
- [ ] Balance against player characters

## Schritt-für-Schritt Erschaffung

### Schritt 1: Konzept und Rolle

**Define your creature's purpose:**
- What is it? (animal, monster, sapient species, undead, etc.)
- Where does it live? (forest, dungeon, mountains, urban areas)
- What does it eat? (carnivore, herbivore, souls, magic)
- How does it interact with the world? (solitary, pack hunter, hive mind, tribal)
- Why would characters encounter it? (random encounter, quest target, ally, obstacle)

### Schritt 2: Intelligenztyp Bestimmen

**Sapient Creatures (INT 8+):**
- Use INT characteristic (2d6+6 for human-level intelligence)
- Can reason, problem-solve, use tactics
- May have culture, language, professions
- Can be player characters in some campaigns

**Instinct-Driven Creatures:**
- Use INS (Instinct) instead of INT
- Fixed INT based on creature type (see table below)
- Calculate INS as 2d6 + fixed INT
- Act on instinct, innate cunning, survival behaviors

#### Creature INT Table

| Fixed INT | Creature Type | Examples |
|-----------|---------------|----------|
| 1 | Invertebrates | Worms, snails, jellyfish |
| 2 | Insects and Crustaceans | Giant ants, scorpions, crabs |
| 3 | Amphibians and Fish | Frogs, sharks, giant lizards |
| 4 | Herbivores (Reptile/Bird/Mammal) | Aurochs, triceratops, deer |
| 5 | Carnivores (Reptile/Bird) | Allosaurus, terror birds |
| 6 | Carnivores (Mammal) | Bears, lions, wolves |
| 7 | Almost Sapient | Apes, chimera, dolphins |
| 8+ | Sapient | Humans, elves, dwarves, dragons |

### Schritt 3: Größe und Eigenschaften

#### A. Determine Size (SIZ)

**Size Categories:**
- **Tiny (SIZ 1-5):** Rat, cat, small dog
- **Small (SIZ 6-10):** Large dog, baboon, child
- **Human-Sized (SIZ 11-20):** Adult human, wolf, deer
- **Large (SIZ 21-30):** Horse, bear, lion
- **Huge (SIZ 31-40):** Aurochs, elephant, small dragon
- **Enormous (SIZ 41-50):** Large dragon, giant, mammoth
- **Colossal (SIZ 51+):** Sea serpent, ancient dragon, kaiju

**Formulas by Size:**
- Human-sized: 2d6+6 (average 13)
- Larger creatures: Add fixed bonus (e.g., 2d6+24 for huge creatures)
- Smaller creatures: Reduce dice (e.g., 1d6+6 for small creatures)

**Important:** The SIZ Equivalency table is NOT linear. Use fewer dice for very large creatures to avoid massive variance.

#### B. Assign Other Characteristics

**STR (Strength):**
- Doesn't need to equal SIZ (strength-to-mass ratio decreases as creatures grow)
- Small creatures: 1d6+3 to 2d6+6
- Human-sized: 2d6+6 to 3d6
- Large creatures: 2d6+12 to 4d6+24
- Keep reasonable to maintain survivability for armored humans

**CON (Constitution):**
- Determines Hit Points and Endurance
- Most creatures: 2d6+6 to 3d6+6
- Tough creatures: 3d6+6 to 4d6+12
- Fragile creatures: 2d6 to 2d6+3

**DEX (Dexterity):**
- Fast/agile creatures: 3d6+6 to 4d6+6
- Average: 2d6+6 to 3d6
- Slow/sluggish: 2d6 to 2d6+3
- Many-limbed creatures get higher DEX

**POW (Power):**
- Mundane animals: 1d6 to 2d6
- Average creatures: 2d6 to 3d6
- Magical creatures: 3d6 to 4d6+6
- Determines Magic Points (equals POW)

**CHA (Charisma):**
- Only important for sapient, social creatures
- Represents personality, not appearance
- Average: 2d6 to 3d6
- Adjust if creature is especially charismatic or alien

### Schritt 4: Attribute Berechnen

**Action Points:**
- INT/INS 6-12 and DEX 6-12: 2 AP
- INT/INS 13-18 or DEX 13-18: 3 AP
- INT/INS 13-18 and DEX 13-18: 4 AP

**Damage Modifier:**
- Lookup table based on STR + SIZ combined
- Small creatures (combined 5 or less): -1d8
- Human range (12-20): -1d6 to +0
- Large creatures (30-40): +1d6 to +1d10
- Huge creatures (50+): +1d12 to +2d8

**Magic Points:**
- Equals POW characteristic

**Movement:**
- Human baseline: 6m
- Slow creatures: 4m
- Fast/quadruped: 8-12m
- Very fast: 15m+
- Flyers: Usually 12m (flying)
- Swimmers: Based on body type

**Initiative Bonus:**
- (INT or INS + DEX) / 2
- Use INS for instinct-driven creatures

### Schritt 5: Fähigkeiten Wählen (1-3 Maximum)

**Choose abilities that fit the creature's nature:**

For detailed mechanical explanations of each ability, see the **Creature Abilities Reference** document.

#### Movement Abilities
- **Adhering:** Spiders, some lizards
- **Aquatic:** Fish, water elementals
- **Burrower:** Moles, earth elementals, umber hulks
- **Flying:** Birds, dragons, winged creatures
- **Swimmer:** Aquatic creatures, amphibians

#### Sensory Abilities
- **Blood Sense:** Sharks, vampires
- **Dark Sight:** Underground dwellers, nocturnal predators
- **Death Sense:** Undead, necromantic creatures
- **Earth Sense:** Underground creatures
- **Echolocation:** Bats, cave dwellers
- **Life Sense:** Nature spirits, undead hunters
- **Magic Sense:** Magical creatures, mage hunters
- **Night Sight:** Nocturnal creatures

#### Combat Abilities
- **Diving Strike:** Flying/aquatic predators
- **Formidable Natural Weapons:** Creatures with horn/chitin that can parry weapons
- **Grappler:** Constrictors, octopi, things with tentacles
- **Intimidate:** Pack leaders, large predators
- **Leaper:** Pouncing predators (lions, spiders)
- **Trample:** Large herbivores, elephants

#### Offensive Abilities
- **Breathe Flame:** Dragons, salamanders
- **Characteristic Drain:** Undead, wraiths, shadows
- **Engulfing:** Creatures that swallow prey whole
- **Gaze Attack:** Basilisks, medusas
- **Venomous:** Snakes, spiders, scorpions

#### Defensive Abilities
- **Camouflaged:** Chameleons, ambush predators
- **Disease Immunity:** Most supernatural creatures
- **Frenzy:** Berserkers, rabid animals
- **Hold Breath:** Aquatic creatures, diving animals
- **Immunity:** Specific damage types (fire, cold, iron, blades)
- **Poison Immunity:** Many undead, constructs
- **Regeneration:** Trolls, hydras, some demons

#### Special Abilities
- **Allergy:** Specific substance causes damage
- **Chaos Aura:** Chaos-tainted creatures
- **Chaos Feature:** Random chaotic mutations
- **Cold-Blooded:** Reptiles, some monsters
- **Communal Mind:** Hive creatures, swarms
- **Multi-Headed:** Hydras, ettins
- **Multi-Limbed:** Spiders, centaurs with weapons
- **Possession (Covert):** Spirits, demons
- **Possession (Dominant):** Possessing spirits
- **Terrifying:** Especially horrific creatures
- **Undead:** Zombies, skeletons, vampires
- **Vampiric:** Blood drainers
- **Vulnerable:** Weak to specific sources

**Recommendation:** Most creatures should have 1-3 abilities maximum. More than 3 makes them overly complex.

### Schritt 6: Fertigkeiten Zuweisen

**Every creature should have:**
- Athletics
- Brawn
- Endurance
- Evade
- Willpower

**Add relevant skills based on nature:**
- Perception (most creatures)
- Stealth (ambush predators)
- Track (hunters)
- Swim (aquatic/amphibious)
- Fly (flying creatures)
- Survival (wilderness creatures)

**Skill Values:**
- Calculate base (STR+DEX for Athletics, etc.)
- Add +20-40% for a typical young adult
- Add +40-60% for veteran/experienced specimens
- Add +60-80% for legendary/ancient examples

**Example Calculations:**
- Giant Spider DEX 16, STR 18: Athletics base = 34%, typical = 34+30 = 64%
- Wolf INS 13, POW 10: Willpower base = 23%, typical = 23+25 = 48%

### Schritt 7: Kampfstil und Waffen Entwerfen

**Combat Style:**
- Name it descriptively (e.g., "Savage Bite," "Pack Hunter," "Aerial Assault")
- List all weapons included in the style
- Set percentage: Base (STR+DEX) + 20-60%

**Natural Weapons:**
- Determine damage based on SIZ (see table)
- Assign Size and Reach
- Select appropriate special effects

#### Natural Weapon Damage by SIZ

| Creature SIZ | Bash/Crush/Claw | Bite/Stab |
|--------------|-----------------|-----------|
| 1-10 | 1d3 | 1d4 |
| 11-20 | 1d4 | 1d6 |
| 21-30 | 1d6 | 1d8 |
| 31-40 | 1d8 | 1d10 |
| 41-50 | 1d10 | 1d12 |
| 51-60 | 1d12 | 2d6 |

#### Natural Weapon Special Effects

| Weapon Type | Available Effects |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Beak (Pointed) | Impale |
| Beak (Tearing) | Bleed, Grip |
| Claws/Talons | Bleed, Grip |
| Horns | Impale |
| Teeth (Crushing) | Grip |
| Teeth (Grasping) | Grip, Impale |
| Teeth (Incising) | Bleed |
| Stinger | Impale |
| Tentacle | Grip, Take Weapon |
| Tail | Bash, Entangle, Grip |

### Schritt 8: Rüstung und Trefferzonen Bestimmen

**Armor (Natural):**
- Fur/Hide: 1-2 AP
- Thick Hide: 2-3 AP
- Scales: 3-4 AP
- Chitin/Shell: 4-6 AP
- Bone Plates: 5-8 AP
- Stone/Metal: 6-10 AP

**Note:** Natural armor has NO armor penalty to Initiative.

**Hit Locations:**
- **Humanoid:** Standard human locations (1-20)
- **Quadruped:** Roll 1d20+10 when attacking from front (limits to forequarters)
- **Winged:** Special table for flying creatures
- **Serpentine:** Tail, body sections, head
- **Multi-legged:** Additional leg locations (spiders, insects)

### Schritt 9: Magie (Optional)

**Folk Magic:**
- Common for sapient creatures
- Skill percentage: POW x2 + 20-40%
- Choose 3-6 thematically appropriate spells

**Theism/Mysticism/Sorcery:**
- Rare, usually only for experienced/legendary specimens
- Makes creatures significantly more dangerous
- Balance carefully against party capabilities

**Innate Magic:**
- Some creatures have magical abilities (breath weapons, gaze attacks)
- These are abilities, not spells
- Usually limited use (once per hour/day)

### Schritt 10: Balance gegen Charaktere

**Ask yourself:**

1. **Abilities:** Do the creature's special abilities give it too much advantage?
   - Reduce number of abilities or encounter numbers

2. **Skills:** Compare Combat Style to party's average
   - Equal to slightly higher (+10-20%): Challenging but fair
   - Much higher (+30-40%): Reduce numbers or make it a boss

3. **Damage Output:** Can the creature one-shot characters?
   - High damage = reduce numbers or Hit Points
   - Consider armor penetration

4. **Durability:** How long does it take to kill?
   - High HP + High Armor = reduce numbers significantly
   - Glass cannon (low HP, high damage) = can have more numbers

5. **Magic:** Can it spell-spam the party?
   - Magic users should be rare/singular
   - Balance spell selection carefully

6. **Numbers:** How many should characters face?
   - Weaker than party: 2-3 to 1 odds
   - Equal to party: 1 to 1 or slightly less
   - Stronger than party: Party should outnumber creature 2-3 to 1

7. **Tactics:** How will it fight?
   - Hit and run, ambush, pack tactics change difficulty dramatically
   - Consider environment advantages

## Machtstufen-Richtlinien

### Bedrohungsstufen

Use this as a guideline for balancing encounters:

#### Level 1: Trivial (Nuisance)
**Examples:** Giant rats, small snakes, normal animals
- Combat Style: 30-45%
- Armor: 0-2 AP
- Abilities: 0-1
- HP per location: 2-4
- Tactics: Flee when wounded
- **Against Party:** 3-5 to 1 odds

#### Level 2: Minor Threat (Challenging)
**Examples:** Wolves, ghouls, giant spiders (small)
- Combat Style: 45-60%
- Armor: 1-3 AP
- Abilities: 1-2
- HP per location: 4-6
- Tactics: Pack tactics, some intelligence
- **Against Party:** 1.5 to 1 odds or 1 to 1

#### Level 3: Moderate Threat (Dangerous)
**Examples:** Bears, trolls, minotaurs, young dragons
- Combat Style: 55-70%
- Armor: 3-5 AP
- Abilities: 2-3
- HP per location: 6-9
- Tactics: Intelligent tactics, use environment
- **Against Party:** 1 to 1 or party outnumbers slightly

#### Level 4: Major Threat (Deadly)
**Examples:** Giants, adult dragons, vampires, powerful sorcerers
- Combat Style: 65-80%
- Armor: 4-7 AP
- Abilities: 2-4 (powerful ones)
- HP per location: 8-12
- Tactics: Sophisticated tactics, magic, prepared defenses
- **Against Party:** Party should outnumber 2-3 to 1

#### Level 5: Epic Threat (Campaign Boss)
**Examples:** Ancient dragons, demon lords, arch-liches
- Combat Style: 75-95%
- Armor: 6-10+ AP
- Abilities: 3-5 (very powerful)
- HP per location: 10-15+
- Tactics: Master tactician, legendary magic, minions
- **Against Party:** Should be solo or with minimal support; requires planning

### Macht-Budget

Think of each enhancement as adding to a "power budget":

| Enhancement | Power Cost |
|-------------|------------|
| +10% to Combat Style | +1 |
| +1 AP (armor) | +1 |
| +1 Special Ability (minor) | +2 |
| +1 Special Ability (major) | +3 |
| +1 Action Point | +3 |
| Magic capability | +2 to +5 |
| Regeneration | +3 to +5 |
| Immunity (specific) | +2 to +4 |

**Guideline Totals:**
- Minor Threat: 3-6 power
- Moderate Threat: 7-12 power
- Major Threat: 13-20 power
- Epic Threat: 21+ power

Balance by reducing numbers encountered or giving weaknesses.

## Beispiel-Kreaturerschaffung

Let's create a new creature step-by-step: **The Shadowmaw**

### Concept

**Idea:** A wolf-sized predator that hunts in darkness, feeds on fear, and can blend into shadows. Lives in ancient ruins and caves.

**Role:** Ambush predator, moderate threat, creates atmosphere of dread

### Schritt-für-Schritt Erschaffung

#### 1. Intelligence Type
- Instinct-driven creature (mammalian carnivore)
- Fixed INT: 6
- INS: 2d6+6 (average 13)

#### 2. Size Category
- Slightly larger than a wolf
- SIZ: 2d6+9 (average 16)

#### 3. Characteristics

| Characteristic | Dice | Average | Reasoning |
|----------------|------|---------|-----------|
| STR | 3d6+3 | 14 | Strong for its size (predator) |
| CON | 3d6+6 | 17 | Tough, resilient |
| SIZ | 2d6+9 | 16 | Large wolf-sized |
| DEX | 3d6+9 | 20 | Very agile (ambush predator) |
| INS | 2d6+6 | 13 | Cunning hunter |
| POW | 3d6+3 | 14 | Magical nature (shadow affinity) |
| CHA | - | - | Not applicable |

#### 4. Derived Attributes

- **Action Points:** 3 (INS 13, DEX 20)
- **Damage Modifier:** +1d2 (STR+SIZ = 30)
- **Magic Points:** 14
- **Movement:** 10m (fast quadruped)
- **Initiative Bonus:** +17 ((13+20)/2, round up)
- **Armor:** Tough Hide (2 AP)

#### 5. Special Abilities (Choose 3)

1. **Camouflaged** - Blends into shadows (-2 grades to Perception)
2. **Intimidate** - Emanates fear aura
3. **Night Sight** - Sees perfectly in darkness

*Reasoning: Fits the shadow-hunter theme without being overpowered*

#### 6. Skills

- **Athletics:** 54% (base 34 + 20)
- **Brawn:** 44% (base 24 + 20)
- **Endurance:** 67% (base 37 + 30)
- **Evade:** 70% (base 40 + 30)
- **Perception:** 63% (base 33 + 30)
- **Stealth:** 80% (base 50 + 30)
- **Track:** 53% (base 33 + 20)
- **Willpower:** 64% (base 34 + 30)

#### 7. Combat Style and Weapons

**Combat Style:** Shadow Hunter (Bite, Claws) 70%

**Natural Weapons:**

| Weapon | Size | Reach | Damage | Special Effects | AP/HP |
|--------|------|-------|---------|-----------------|-------|
| Bite | M | T | 1d6+1d2 | Grip, Bleed | 1/6 |
| Claw | M | M | 1d4+1d2 | Bleed, Grip | 1/5 |

#### 8. Hit Locations (Quadruped)

| 1d20 | Location | AP/HP |
|------|----------|-------|
| 1-2 | Right Hind Leg | 2/5 |
| 3-4 | Left Hind Leg | 2/5 |
| 5-7 | Hindquarters | 2/6 |
| 8-10 | Forequarters | 2/7 |
| 11-13 | Right Front Leg | 2/5 |
| 14-16 | Left Front Leg | 2/5 |
| 17-20 | Head | 2/6 |

#### 9. Special Mechanics

**Shadow Aura:** When in dim light or darkness, the shadowmaw can spend 1 Magic Point to become nearly invisible for 1d3 rounds. During this time, Perception rolls to spot it are at Herculean difficulty. The effect ends if it attacks or enters bright light.

**Fear Feeding:** When the shadowmaw successfully uses its Intimidate ability and causes a character to flee, it regains 1d3 Magic Points as it feeds on the fear.

#### 10. Tactics and Behavior

- **Ambush Hunter:** Waits in shadows, uses Shadow Aura to approach
- **Intimidate First:** Uses fear aura to scatter groups, picks off stragglers
- **Hit and Run:** Makes one attack then retreats to shadows
- **Flee When Wounded:** If reduced to 1/2 HP, will disengage and flee
- **Pack Tactics:** Rarely encountered in groups larger than 2-3

#### 11. Balance Check

**Power Budget:**
- Combat Style 70%: +3 power
- 3 Abilities (Camouflaged, Intimidate, Night Sight): +6 power
- Shadow Aura special: +2 power
- **Total: 11 power** = Moderate Threat

**Against Party of 4:**
- 1 Shadowmaw = Challenging encounter
- 2 Shadowmaws = Dangerous encounter
- 3 Shadowmaws = Deadly encounter

**Weaknesses:**
- Bright light negates camouflage and Shadow Aura
- Moderate armor (can be penetrated)
- Flees when wounded (can be driven off)

## Shadowmaw

A wolf-sized predator covered in midnight-black fur that seems to absorb light. Its eyes glow with a faint red luminescence, and wisps of shadow trail from its body. Shadowmaws haunt ancient ruins and deep caves, feeding on the fear of those who venture into darkness. They are intelligent enough to use sophisticated ambush tactics and can blend seamlessly into shadows, becoming nearly invisible.

Shadowmaws hunt in small packs of 2-3, using their Intimidate ability to scatter groups before picking off isolated victims. They prefer hit-and-run tactics, striking from darkness and retreating before prey can mount an effective defense. If seriously wounded, a shadowmaw will flee rather than fight to the death.

## Häufige Fehler Vermeiden

### 1. **Too Many Abilities**
❌ **Don't:** Give creatures 5-6 special abilities
✅ **Do:** Limit to 1-3 abilities that define the creature

### 2. **Unbeatable Defense**
❌ **Don't:** High armor + high HP + regeneration + immunity
✅ **Do:** Pick 2 defensive features max, give weaknesses

### 3. **Rocket Tag Combat**
❌ **Don't:** Massive damage + high combat skill + multi-attack
✅ **Do:** Balance offense and defense; high damage = lower accuracy or HP

### 4. **No Tactical Depth**
❌ **Don't:** "It attacks until dead"
✅ **Do:** Give creatures behavior patterns, retreat conditions, pack tactics

### 5. **Ignoring Environment**
❌ **Don't:** Create creatures without considering habitat
✅ **Do:** Design abilities and tactics that leverage environment

### 6. **Power Creep**
❌ **Don't:** Make each creature stronger than the last
✅ **Do:** Use the power budget; variety > raw power

### 7. **Forgetting Weaknesses**
❌ **Don't:** Create creatures with no vulnerabilities
✅ **Do:** Every strength should have a corresponding weakness

### 8. **Magic Spam**
❌ **Don't:** Give creatures unlimited spell access
✅ **Do:** Limit spell selection, give cooldowns on powerful abilities

### 9. **Arbitrary Stats**
❌ **Don't:** "This creature has 90% combat skill because it's scary"
✅ **Do:** Follow progression guidelines based on age/experience

### 10. **One-Dimensional Design**
❌ **Don't:** Make creatures that only exist for combat
✅ **Do:** Give them motivations, behaviors, ecology, interesting lore
